Use the interactive charts below to explore the dataset

Starting in 2011, a group of scientists at the NC State Public Science Lab launched the Belly Button Biodiversity Project to investigate the diverse microbes inhabiting our navels. Hundreds of people participated by sending samples of belly button microbes from sterile swab collections. What started as a small study turned into a large undertaking as people from all across the country participated. Each sample was grown in a dish and later genetically analyzed to determine what microbial species were present. Data for the participants are shown here and can be accessed by selecting the participant's test ID number from the upper left panel. For full details see: The Belly Button Biodiversity Project History.
